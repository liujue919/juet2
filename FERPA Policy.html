FERPA POLICY

 

RESTRICTION OF AUDIO OR VISUAL RECORDING, REPRODUCTION, AND DISTRIBUTION OF CONTENT IN COURSES

At LIBI, we protect the intellectual property of all our faculty, and safeguard the privacy of all our students in all learning environments. To protect the rights and safety of all members of our community, including copyright protection for third-party vendors who provide online textbooks used solely for in-class lectures, students may not record, reproduce, screenshot, photograph, or distribute any video, audio, or visual content from their courses taught in-person or remotely. This restriction includes but is not limited to the distribution of:

• Pre-recorded and live in-class lectures;

• Live discussions;

• Discussion boards and other social media sites;

• Posted course materials;

• Class and homework assignments, quizzes, tests, midterm exams, and final exams;

• Visual materials that accompany lectures/discussions, such as PowerPoint slides.

As we participate in remote learning, it is of paramount importance that all members of this college community are treated with respect. Remote learning carries additional privacy requirements so please bear in mind that if any student is identifiable in an online class recording, this may constitute a violation of the educational record protections provided under FERPA.

Class recordings are available for review only to students enrolled in the course for a given semester. Those recordings may not be shared with anyone outside of that registered course. Class recordings, including any other hard-copy course materials, may not be recorded on cell phones, video cameras, personal laptops, or reproduced in any other format.

ADA exception: Students with disabilities who need to record classroom lectures or discussions must contact the Accessibility Coordinator to register, request, and be approved for an accommodation. All students are advised that students approved for this form of accommodation may record classroom activities. Such recordings are to be used solely for individual or group study with other students enrolled in the class that semester. Such recordings may not be reproduced, shared in any way (including electronically or posting in any web environment) with those not in the class that semester.

Students are expected to abide by this policy or face sanctions that may be subject to both legal ramifications for violations of copyright law and disciplinary action, including permanent dismissal from the college, under the Student Code of Conduct.
